Zilter Web App

UX Development

Description

The goal of this course was to focus on hands-on practice with a modern human-centered design process while building a web application to serve a set of target users. The theme was to enhance recreational activity.

We materialized this goal via Zilter, a web application concept that eases the shopping experience by consolidating all product research into one convenient place. We chose to address the problems of online shopping because it can be a tedious task given that you will want to acquire certain information to get the best product at the best price. Specifically, Zilter provides standard product information, accurate photos and videos, and official reviews pulled from external websites. All sources are consolidated in a bibliography.

Responsibility

My main role was to implement digital mockups into user testable prototypes and in turn, present these to an audience while delivering a pitch for our concept. I shared the responsibility of performing user testing and turning feedback into thoughtful design tweaks.

Need Finding

For the scope of our project to enhance recreation, our team decided to hone in on the opportunity to analyze breakdowns and design opportunities around the online shopping experience.

First, we conducted ad libitum sampling on three users in which the observer informally records all observations that are relevant or interesting. We simply asked the users to shop for an item online.

User 1: Kevin, 19 Years Old

Kevin is a 3rd year biology student who enjoys cooking and needs a whetstone to sharpen his knives and a mesh strainer to filter and to clean his vegetables.

While observing Kevin, I noticed that he needed a way to figure out if a product’s dimensions suited him. Here, he uses the width of a p aper to truly understand the diameter of a strainer. Although measurements were provided, he needed a way to compare it relative to himself. To us, this was a design opportunity to improve product expectations.

User 2: Charles, 21 Years Old

Charles is a 4th year pre-law student who likes to browse and shop for clothes and high-fashion items online during his free time.

After making an offer to a seller on Grailed, a marketplace website, Charles switches over to YouTube to watch videos on the item he was looking at on Grailed. Charles needs to make a better judgement of the item of interest while simultaneously shopping for it. What we took from this observation was that users often need external sources to help them make purchasing decisions.

User 3: Ariane, 20 Years Old

Ariane is a 3rd year math-computer science student who likes to spend her free time browsing through online retail stores looking to see if any pieces catches her attention.

Ariane is browsing through a clothing website and wants to find the best deals. She sets the price filter from lowest to highest so that she can see the items that are on sale or at the best price first. This observation shows us that users want to filter through large quantities of products to find exactly what they are looking for.

In conclusion, after analyzing different users engage in online browsing and shopping, we have deduced that people want the best combination of quality and price. In order to meet this demand, we chose to play the role that informs users instead of markets to users. Essentially, we want to create the Wikipedia of shopping.

Working Prototypes

The final iteration polishes the user interface by incorporating color, images and other visual detail. Functionality was also implemented to unite together the static web pages.

Many websites used for product researching focus on a single aspect of information and do not include an array of necessary data to come to a decision. For example, Youtube presents videos and CNET is generally acknowledged for tech reviews but neither curate sources from other outlets. The concept of Zilter takes key components from all over the internet to curate the most relevant content for users to access on one platform. After filtering out specifics, users click on a result to see photos and all the technical specifications as well as critical reviews and videos that help them make the best choice.

Branding

The name was inspired by the physical functionality of a filter. Like a filter, we hope that our web app removes unwanted noise when trying to separate out important and non-important information.

The black and gray color scheme was chosen to reflect the simplicity of our mission goal of making product purchases straightforward. The focus of our site is the products themselves so minimal attention ought to go towards anything else. The green accents were infused to add a splash of color representing the excitement of making a purchase and to also build brand identity. Furthermore, the black and green combination is reminiscent of old-school computer terminals which then brings us to the new age of information that our web app presents. The Zilter logo accentuates the letter Z by shaping itself into the silhouette of a lightning bolt which symbolizes fast access to information at one’s fingertips that we hope to provide users in order to help them make an informed purchase.

Conclusion + Takeaways

Online shopping was a recreational activity that we wanted to enhance. To do this, we first had to understand what about shopping that could be improved on. After observing real people shop, we came up with a solution that bridges the gap between wanting a product and actually purchasing it: knowing which one to purchase. With this information hub, users can come with their questions and leave with a clear consumer intention.
